Fantasy Week 1

The Afternoon Delights got off to a fast start with a score of 134.16, enough to win Week 1's $40 High Score prize. And their opponent, Farmer Fran, got the 2nd highest score of the week in the loss, which nets them the Bad Beat prize, which had gotten all the way up to a whopping $15. The Bad Beat is expected to only hit 0-4 times per year, so it's pretty surprising that it hit in week 1.

The Hulkamaniacs fell to Hall despite Saquon Barkley's lackluster game. The Hulkamaniacs are now the first non-Tommy-owned team to be subject to a $10 Tommy Rule Fine for starting Kenny Golladay, who was ruled OUT for the game. The fine pool is awared to the team with the most points against at the end of the season (Which was Tommy last year, unfortunately). The Bareback Barback managed to outscore their projected total and still lose by 13 to Team America. Tom Brady played so bad that Giselle reviewed their pre-nup, but he still managed 24 points for Barback. But, Russell Wilson dominated Atlanta for Team America, after quietly having an excellent 2019 season that was overshadowed by Lamar Jackson's MVP season. The Morrell Streetfighters (Ryan -- I guess name trade negotions fell through) lost to a team that almost didn't show up for the draft. The Ed Horne Football Team lost a close one to Team Uncle Bill. Both teams appear to be sticking with the names the commisioner put in for their team when inviting them to the league, 2 weeks and 16 years ago, respectively. The Crackers were the lowest scoring winner in a defeat of the Crusaders. The Crackers finished playing on Sunday and were projected to lose a close one, but the Crusader's 3 Monday players could not get the job done.

Reports indicate that the league office is in serious talks to procure a new site for the 2021 fantasy draft. It's potentially the farthest drive for a fantasy draft yet.
