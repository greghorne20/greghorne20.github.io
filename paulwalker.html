DRAFT ORDER BEFORE KEEPER SELECTIONS

When we approved adding a keeper at last year's draft, it was stated that each team's keeper would be selected before the draft order was determined. This proposes changing the order of those two events. The draft order would be determined before keepers are selected.

FINES FOR STARTING INACTIVE PLAYERS

Some owners who were essentially out of the playoff race have neglected to set their lineups. This can hurt potential playoff teams by giving competing potential playoff teams easier wins. Here are some examples from last year of teams which left inactive players in:

  • Week 15 - Tommy started Carson Wentz, who was injured and did not play.
  • Week 12 - Tommy started Ryan Fitzpatrick, who did not play. It was reported on the Tuesday of that week that Winston would start.
  • Week 10 - Tommy started the Texans D/ST. The Texans had a bye week.
  • Week 8 - Tommy left his tight end postion empty. Jared Cook scored 15.4 on the bench.
  • Week 4 - Tommy started Corey Clement, who was declared inactive 90 minutes before kickoff.
  • Week 4 - Tommy left his kicker position empty.

This rule proposes that any team owner who leaves a starting slot empty or with a player who has been declared inactive will incur a $10 fine for each postion (e.g. Tommy would have 2 fines in Week 4). If a player does not play, but was one of the 46 active players, there is no violation.

At the end of the season, the team with the most points against will get the fine pool.

CHANGE TO LEAGUE DUES

Based on the current league rules, the league fee would increase by $5 to $65 this year. This rule change proposes changing the league fee. Voting will be done via an Instant Runoff. That means you need to rank all of the choices by your preference.

  • A: No change - $65 with a $5 increase each year.
  • B: $70 and change the increase to $10 each year.
  • C: $75 and eliminate the automatic increase.
  • D: $100 and eliminate the automatic increase.

TRADE VETOES

Currently, only cleary unfair trades are subject to veto soley at the commisioner's discretion. This is intended for anti-competitive trades only. Allowing a simple majority to veto a trade is stupid. Then it would make sense for every team to veto most trades to benefit their own interests. Therefore, this rule proposes adding a veto vote that requires a supermajority to reverse clearly unfair trades. After two teams make a trade, another team can call for a veto vote. At least 65% of the uninvolved teams must vote to veto for the trade to be reversed. (e.g. 7 out of 10 for typical 2 team trades; 6 of 9 for 3 team trades; 6 of 8 for 4 team trades; etc.)

Or we can stick with the current system. (The LORD is good and does what is right; he shows the proper path to those who go astray. [Psalms 25:8])

This proposal is the result of whining after a Crusaders trade last year.
